How to Rewrite AI Text to Sound Human

Core Insight: AI detectors measure two things above all — predictability
(how expected each word is) and burstiness (how much sentence length
varies). Every phase below targets one or both.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 1 — BREAK SENTENCE UNIFORMITY (Burstiness)
────────────────────────────────────────
AI writes in balanced, complete sentences. Deliberately break this:

- Split one long sentence into two shorter ones.
- Occasionally merge two short ones into one longer, slightly awkward one.
- Start some sentences mid-thought, as if continuing from memory.
- After two or three medium sentences, drop in a very short one. Even
  a fragment.
- Target: no two consecutive sentences should share the same rough length.
  Vary between 5-word and 30-word sentences within the same paragraph.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 2 — DOWNGRADE VOCABULARY SELECTIVELY (Predictability)
────────────────────────────────────────
Replace formal/precise AI words with simpler or slightly imprecise human
equivalents — but not uniformly. Patchy formality is more human than
consistent simplicity:

- "cardiovascular disease" → "heart disease"
- "frequently" → "often" or "a lot of the time"
- "abnormalities" → "problems" or "changes"
- "associated with" → "linked to" or just "from"
- "individuals" → "people"
- "utilize" → "use"
- "optimal" → "best" or "ideal" (keep one "optimal" — don't scrub all)
- "demonstrate" → "show"
- "significant" → "real" or "major" or drop the qualifier entirely

Leave 30–40% of formal words intact. Uniform downgrading looks robotic
in its own way.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 3 — DESTROY NOMINALIZATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────
AI loves turning verbs into noun phrases. Humans usually don't.

Spot them as "the \_\_\_-tion/-ment/-ance of" constructions and convert back
to verb form:

- "the implementation of new policies" → "implementing new policies"
- "the development of trust" → "building trust"
- "the reduction of inflammation" → "reducing inflammation"
- "the maintenance of records" → "keeping records"

This one change alone meaningfully raises unpredictability scores.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 4 — REMOVE ELEGANT TRANSITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────
AI loves openers like:

- "Despite this..."
- "Furthermore..."
- "This highlights the importance of..."
- "It is worth noting that..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "Notably..."

Delete or replace with blunter connections — or just remove the transition
entirely and let ideas sit next to each other without a bridge. Humans
trust readers to follow a logical jump. AI over-explains the connection.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 5 — BREAK PARALLEL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────
AI is extremely consistent with parallel grammatical structure in lists
and multi-part sentences. Humans are not.

- AI: "The plan improved efficiency, reduced costs, and increased morale."
- Human: "The plan made things more efficient, cut costs, and staff
  seemed happier."

Deliberately mismatch verb forms, tense, or structure in at least one
list or multi-part sentence per paragraph.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 6 — INTRODUCE CONTROLLED IMPERFECTION
────────────────────────────────────────

- Repeat a word or short phrase within the same paragraph (humans do this).
- Use a slightly redundant clause — e.g., "tools and training" appearing
  twice nearby is fine.
- Leave one sentence slightly less resolved than ideal.
- Add one mild hedge mid-paragraph: "which probably explains," "at least
  in part," "or something close to it." Don't overdo it — one per section.
- Occasionally start a sentence with "And" or "But." Humans do.
  Style guides say not to; people do it anyway.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 7 — VARY CLAUSE AND LOGIC ORDER
────────────────────────────────────────
AI structures information in the most logical order. Shuffle it:

- Move a qualifier to an unexpected position mid-sentence.
- Put the consequence before the cause occasionally.
- Nest a detail inside a sentence rather than listing it cleanly.
- Occasionally lead with the specific example before the general point,
  instead of the reverse (which is what AI almost always does).

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 8 — INJECT SPECIFICITY (NEW)
────────────────────────────────────────
AI tends toward vague generality. Humans anchor claims with specifics,
even casual ones:

- Replace "studies have shown" with "a 2021 study out of Johns Hopkins"
  or even just "researchers found."
- Replace "in many cases" with "in most cases" or "in a few situations"
  — pick a direction.
- Replace "some people" with "a lot of people" or "most people" —
  commit slightly more than AI does.

Specificity, even imprecise specificity, raises the human signal.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 9 — ADJUST PARAGRAPH RHYTHM (NEW)
────────────────────────────────────────
AI produces paragraphs of similar length. Break this:

- Let one paragraph run longer than feels comfortable.
- Follow it with a 1–2 sentence paragraph.
- Vary where the "topic sentence" sits — sometimes bury the main point
  mid-paragraph instead of leading with it.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 10 — BREAK TRICOLONS AND PARALLEL LISTS
────────────────────────────────────────
AI defaults to three-part parallel lists. They are one of the
strongest detectable patterns.

- "identifying things, weighing options, reaching conclusions"
  → Drop to two items, or break the parallelism:
  → "identifying things and figuring out which option makes sense"
- "an input layer, one or more hidden layers, and an output layer"
  → "an input layer, then hidden layers in between, then an output"
- Never let a three-part list remain where all three items share
  the same grammatical form. Break at least one.
- If a list must stay, make one item noticeably longer than the others.

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 11 — DISRUPT MIRRORED CONDITIONAL PAIRS
────────────────────────────────────────
AI loves clean When/If or If/Otherwise paired logic:

- "When X happens, Y occurs. If it doesn't, nothing moves forward."

This mirrored structure is highly detectable. Fix by:

- Collapsing both sides into one sentence with "otherwise" buried
  mid-clause
- Dropping one side entirely and letting the reader infer the negative
- Making one side more specific and the other more vague
  → "When a node crosses its threshold, it fires and passes data along —
  anything below that just doesn't register."

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 12 — REMOVE MID-SENTENCE SELF-EXPLANATION
────────────────────────────────────────
AI frequently inserts a clause that explains the clause before it:

- "...manual feature extraction, meaning someone has to define
  what the model should look for."
- "...activates and passes data to the next layer, which is how
  information moves through the network."

These "meaning / which means / that is / in other words" insertions
are a strong AI signal. Humans trust the reader to follow. Either:

- Delete the explanatory clause entirely
- Move the explanation to its own separate sentence, slightly reworded
- Replace with a blunter phrasing that doesn't re-explain

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 13 — VARY PARAGRAPH INTERNAL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────
AI paragraphs follow a predictable logic arc:
claim → expand → contrast or example → resolution

Detectors analyze this at the paragraph level, not just the sentence
level. Break it by:

- Starting one paragraph with the example before the claim
- Ending one paragraph on an expansion rather than a resolution
  (leave it slightly open)
- Burying the main point in the middle of a paragraph rather
  than leading with it
- Letting one paragraph do only two of the four moves — not all of them

────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 14 — FORCE EXTREME BURSTINESS
────────────────────────────────────────
The current approach varies sentence length moderately. That is not
enough. Push to the extremes:

- Every 3–4 sentences, insert one that is 5 words or fewer.
  A fragment is fine.
- Every paragraph should contain at least one sentence over 35 words
  that slightly runs on.
- Do not let three consecutive sentences land in the 15–25 word range.
- The short sentence should follow the long one, not precede it.

Example of forced burstiness:
[32 words] Neural networks skip the step where someone manually
defines what features the model should look for — instead they build
up their own representations by moving through the data layer by layer.
[4 words] That happens automatically.
[19 words] It's part of what makes them useful for image data,
where the relevant features aren't always obvious upfront.
